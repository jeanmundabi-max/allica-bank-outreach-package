The Core Leak

Allica’s growth to date has been intermediary-led. Accountants and finance brokers introduce SME clients. The model works — it built Allica from zero to £3 billion in lending and £29.9 million in profit in 2024. But the model has a ceiling. Tripling the customer base through broker relationships alone is not possible. The 10% penetration target by 2027 requires a different kind of acquisition.

HockeyStack was deployed to fix attribution. It worked — last-touch bias was eliminated, upstream touchpoints became visible, and sales-assisted pipeline was correctly credited. But HockeyStack was built to measure targeted campaigns. Right now it is measuring an intermediary acquisition model. The ABM campaigns it should be attributing do not exist at scale.

The sequencing problem: the measurement infrastructure is more mature than the campaign infrastructure it was designed to support. The tool is ready. The campaigns are not. Every month without a running ABM motion, the 10% target depends entirely on broker relationships — and broker relationships grow linearly, not at the pace a post-Series D investor expects.

The gap is not measurement. The gap is the acquisition engine that HockeyStack was built to measure.
What This Seems to Indicate

HockeyStack has no ABM campaigns to measure at scale

The case study confirms Allica moved from last-touch bias to full multi-touch attribution across the customer journey. But the upstream demand-creation activities HockeyStack now tracks are primarily intermediary relationships — not digital ABM campaigns. The tool can see everything. There is currently nothing segment-specific to see.

The intermediary channel cannot reach the next 7%

Allica sits at approximately 3 to 4% penetration of around 1.4 million established UK SMEs. Getting to 10% means adding roughly 80,000 to 90,000 net new SME relationships. Broker referrals grow linearly. ABM targeting by industry, company size, and lifecycle stage is the mechanism that reaches beyond what the intermediary channel delivers.

No segment campaign architecture exists

The SME book likely concentrates in construction, manufacturing, professional services, and hospitality — each with distinct lending profiles, close rates, and messaging requirements. There is no campaign playbook by segment. No HubSpot nurture workflows by segment. No LinkedIn ABM programme by segment. The infrastructure that makes lending growth repeatable across new segments does not exist.

International expansion creates a second acquisition gap

Allica is moving into international markets for the first time. The intermediary relationships that built the UK book do not transfer to new geographies. A digital ABM motion that reaches SME segments directly — without waiting years for broker network development — is the mechanism that makes international expansion viable at the pace the Series D timeline requires.

What MDB Runs First
1

Build the segment architecture and run it as the ABM foundation

Rank SME segments by average lending value, close rate, and product fit. Construction, manufacturing, professional services, hospitality, and healthcare are likely starting points based on Allica’s existing book. Each segment gets its own campaign playbook — message, channel mix, offer, and HockeyStack attribution tag. MDB builds and runs this as the ongoing targeting logic every subsequent ABM campaign operates from.

Produces: The targeting foundation every campaign sits on, plus the data framework that makes HockeyStack attribution meaningful by segment rather than by channel.
2

Run HubSpot ABM workflows for the first two segments

One segment, one nurture workflow, one live A/B test — then a second. MDB instruments and operates the workflows continuously, not as a one-time build. Every lead that enters HockeyStack has a corresponding automated nurture path. The attribution data immediately shows what works at the segment level, creating the benchmark every subsequent campaign improves against.

Produces: Immediate HockeyStack attribution data for the first segments, and a repeatable workflow template that scales to segments three through five.
3

Launch LinkedIn ABM campaigns targeting specific SME segments

LinkedIn targeting by company size, industry, seniority, and geography. For Allica: established UK businesses, 50 to 250 employees, specific industries, business owner or finance director title. MDB runs these campaigns continuously — the running digital acquisition motion that complements the intermediary channel. International expansion gets a dedicated segment campaign architecture from the start.

Produces: The first segment-level pipeline data and the proof that digital ABM attribution works for SME banking at scale.
The Shift

Right now, customer acquisition depends on the intermediary channel and the brand awareness Allica has built over seven years. Both are strong. Neither is targeted enough to reach the specific SME segments where lending profitability is highest, or to move fast enough in new geographies without waiting for broker networks to develop. MDB is the engine that runs the ABM function — the segment architecture, the HubSpot workflows, the LinkedIn campaigns, and the HockeyStack attribution layer that ties it together. The intermediary channel stays strong. MDB adds the digital acquisition motion that runs alongside it.
